Last week, I travelled down to London for a PhD Networking Event organised by The Alan Turing Institute. It was a great chance to get a peek into people's research and, of course, network! 

As expected, a room full of PhD students who all care about a future where AI is used responsibly quickly turned into interesting discussions, creating a space where people openly shared both ideas and PhD struggles.

I really enjoyed the experience, and I'm grateful to be able to attend opportunities like this that make the PhD journey so exciting!

Highlights
------
  - Icebreaker Bingo with squares to be marked off when "meeting someone who" - the topics ranging from hobbies (speaking more than one language, travelling or having read more than 4 books that year and may I remind you this was only two months into the year), stress coping mechanisms, future plans (academia vs industry) and many more - quickly lead to engaging conversations.
  - Lunchtime conversations revealed how people from diverse backgrounds found their way to the same place.
  - After-event social hangout sparked many more exciting discussions.
