I'm an PhD student at the Institute for Safe Autonomy at the University of York.

I completed my M.Sc. at the Technical University Munich and a B.Sc. at the Technical University Berlin with exchanges at KTH - The Royal Institute of Technology, Stockholm and UiT - The Arctic University of Norway, Tromsø.

Previously, I’ve been a Junior Research Scientist at Fraunhofer IKS in Munich.

Motivation
======
While I think that the employment of embodied AI has great potential (e.g. solving some of our problems stemming from shortage of skilled workers, conducting tasks dangerous to humans, ...), I believe that we must improve the safety and trustworthiness of those systems prior to employment.

Below are some of the main research areas I am currently interested in:

- Representation Engineering
- Mechanistic Interpretability for Safety Assurance
- Interpretability-based Safety Monitoring

More specifically, I study the behavior of foundation models, with the goal of understanding and controlling their internal processes. I am particularly interested in vision-language models, where gaps in understanding remain and language provides a natural interface to interact with them. My work focuses in particular on clarifying core concepts and developing sufficient safety monitoring.

Multidisciplinary Approach
------

I believe that such solutions do not only require contributions from Computer Science but also from Domain Experts, Engineering, Law, Math, Phylosophie and Social Science, which is why I chose to pursue my PhD within the [SAINTS (UKRI AI Centre for Doctoral Training in Safe Artificial Intelligence Systems) programme](https://www.saints-cdt.ai/).
